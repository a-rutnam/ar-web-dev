About Me

I am a front-end developer with a particular interest in creating intuitive and beautiful websites that are a pleasure for users to peruse.

Through an immersive course at General Assembly I’ve definitely caught the web-development bug. The challenges of learning a new coding language are more than matched by the sense of accomplishment I feel when something works - whether that involves making the words ‘Hello World’ print in the browser, or building a website that required the user to complete physical exercises (recognised by the Microsoft Kinect) in order to read a news article.

I come from a background of working in office administration and sub-editing that has stood me in good stead for coding (especially when it comes to debugging). Prior to this I obtained a diploma in Applied Fashion, where I was always drawn to computer-aided design (CAD) both in developing clothing patterns and creating fashion illustrations.

I’m eager to keep learning and to become a contributing member of the dynamic developer community.

Projects

Some of the websites I have created and/or collaborated on.

The Daily Move

  • The Daily Move incorporates the Microsoft Kinect in a web app, with users having to complete a series of exercises (catching flying boxes on the screen) in order to access the news story of their choice. The app was created using Ruby on Rails, Javascript, Jquery, P5 and OSCeleton.
Visit The Daily Move

Travelr

  • As part of a team I helped build the Travelr social website which enables users to browse and comment on travel “Spots”. Using the Google Maps and Flickr APIs, users can see photos that have been taken in proximity to a given Spot.
Visit Travelr

Chic Cycle

  • Chic Cycle acts as a catalogue guiding shoppers to vintage fashion listings on Etsy that match a contemporary trend. Future plans for the site include a forum in which users can request trends. Approved users will also be able to nominate Etsy listings as being relevant to a given a trend, as well as create new trend pages themselves.
Visit the Chic Cycle

Tic Tac Toe

  • Building a web application from scratch, without a starter codebase, I mapped out the game logic for the simple game Tic Tac Toe. My program allows two players to compete and random selector decides who plays first. For this game I used HTML5, CSS3, jQuery and Javascript.
Visit TicTacToe
